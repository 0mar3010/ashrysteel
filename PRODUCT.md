# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Contractors, construction companies, procurement managers, and real estate developers researching steel suppliers. They arrive to evaluate credibility (capacity, certifications, track record), check specs/product catalogs, and ultimately request a quote or contact sales. This is a considered B2B purchase decision, not an impulse visit — trust signals matter more than persuasion tricks.

## Product Purpose

A brand/marketing site for ashrysteel, a steel manufacturing/distribution business. The site's job is to establish credibility with industrial buyers and convert research visits into quote requests or sales contact. Success looks like a procurement manager coming away confident in ashrysteel's scale, quality standards, and reliability — enough to pick up the phone or submit an RFQ.

## Brand Personality

Established, trusted, authoritative. The tone is confident industrial competence, not startup energy or decorative flourish — credibility earned through scale and substance (production capacity, certifications, real facilities) rather than claimed through adjectives. Calm, serious, built-to-last.

## Anti-references

Ezz Steel, Qatar Steel, Kuwait Steel, and EMSTEEL were reviewed as category baselines. Their shared visual language — white/light surfaces, blue-branded navigation and accents — is not itself the problem; familiar credibility cues are appropriate for this B2B audience and a considered-purchase context rewards recognition over novelty. What should still be avoided is what actually reads as generic across those sites: hero carousels/sliders, indistinct stock-feeling photography, cluttered stat-dashboard layouts, decorative gradients, glassmorphism, and template-grade identical card grids. Differentiation comes from precision of execution — grid discipline, real photography, typographic clarity, restrained and purposeful color — not from inverting the palette into something moodier than the audience expects.

## Design Principles

- **Credibility through clarity, not decoration**: white space, grid discipline, and structure are the trust signal for this audience — precision reads as competence faster than atmosphere does.
- **Engineering over marketing**: typography and layout should read closer to technical documentation or an annual report than a persuasion-driven landing page.
- **Blue as discipline, not decoration**: the brand blue marks interaction, hierarchy, and trust; it does not flood the surface or substitute for structure.
- **Show, don't tell**: real photography of plants, products, and construction applications over stock imagery, illustration, or abstract iconography.
- **Calm authority over urgency**: this is a considered B2B decision — the design should reassure and inform, not pressure or hype.
- **Substance-first sustainability**: ESG/sustainability content is part of the credibility story for procurement buyers, not a decorative afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA: contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, reduced-motion alternatives for any animation. No additional known requirements at this time.
